# Tech Digest — 2026-05-06

**1. Higher usage limits for Claude and a compute deal with SpaceX**
Anthropic raised Claude usage limits and signed a near-term compute partnership with SpaceX, underscoring that frontier-AI product velocity is still gated by capacity, not just model quality. For engineering leaders, this is another signal to treat inference supply, quota strategy, and vendor concentration as first-class planning risks. [Read](https://www.anthropic.com/news/higher-limits-spacex)

**2. Vibe coding and agentic engineering are getting closer than I'd like**
Simon Willison argues that “vibe coding” and disciplined agentic engineering are starting to blur as coding agents become more capable. The practical takeaway is that teams need explicit review, evaluation, and ownership norms before agent-written code becomes an invisible production dependency. [Read](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/6/vibe-coding-and-agentic-engineering/)

**3. How to Work and Compound with AI**
Eugene Yan frames effective AI work as context infrastructure, taste-as-configuration, verification loops, and delegation that compounds over time. It is a useful operating model for turning individual AI productivity into repeatable engineering leverage instead of scattered prompt tricks. [Read](https://eugeneyan.com/writing/working-with-ai/)

**4. Introducing Google Cloud Fraud Defense, the next evolution of reCAPTCHA**
Google is repositioning reCAPTCHA as a broader fraud-defense platform for an “agentic web,” where bots may look more like delegated user agents than simple scripts. Security and platform teams should watch this space because identity, abuse prevention, and bot policy are being redesigned around AI-mediated traffic. [Read](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/identity-security/introducing-google-cloud-fraud-defense-the-next-evolution-of-recaptcha/)

**5. Go is FIPS 140-3 certified**
Go’s cryptographic module now has FIPS 140-3 certification, reducing compliance friction for teams building regulated backend systems in Go. This matters most for enterprises that previously needed wrappers, alternate crypto modules, or extra certification work to deploy Go services in government, finance, and healthcare environments. [Read](https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-module-validation-program/certificate/5247)
