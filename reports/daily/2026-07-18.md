# Tech Digest — 2026-07-18

**1. Anthropic’s playbook for AI-driven code migrations**

Anthropic details migrations spanning tens of thousands to one million lines, including Bun’s two-week Zig-to-Rust port, using phased agents, parity checks, adversarial reviews, and existing test suites. The lesson for engineering leaders is that agentic rewrites can now compress years into weeks—but only when rigorous verification is designed into the workflow. [Read](https://claude.com/blog/ai-code-migration)

**2. Thinking Machines releases Inkling, a 975B open-weights multimodal model**

Inkling is a Mixture-of-Experts model with 41B active parameters, a one-million-token context window, and native text, image, and audio reasoning; its weights are available for customization. It offers teams a serious open foundation for domain-specific multimodal systems without committing entirely to closed-model APIs. [Read](https://thinkingmachines.ai/news/introducing-inkling/)

**3. Secure Boot was bypassable for most of its existence**

Researchers found that old, vulnerable Linux shims remained Microsoft-signed and unrevoked, enabling a relatively simple Secure Boot bypass on both Windows and Linux devices. This is a stark supply-chain lesson: cryptographic trust is only as strong as revocation hygiene and lifecycle ownership. [Read](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/07/microsoft-secure-boot-has-been-broken-for-most-of-its-existence/)

**4. Slack’s next-generation EC2 platform brings immutable rollouts to VMs**

Slack’s Shipyard replaces drift-prone, long-lived instance updates with immutable images, progressive deployment, and automated safety across tens of thousands of EC2 instances. It is a useful blueprint for modernizing VM-heavy estates when a wholesale container migration is impractical. [Read](https://slack.engineering/shipyard-how-we-built-slacks-next-generation-ec2-platform/)

**5. Speech recognition and synthesis fit in 470 KB of RAM**

Moonshine Micro runs voice activity detection, command recognition, and neural speech synthesis on an 80-cent RP2350 microcontroller with as little as 470 KB of RAM. This pushes private, low-latency voice interfaces into embedded products where cloud inference is too costly, slow, or unreliable. [Read](https://github.com/moonshine-ai/moonshine/tree/main/micro)
