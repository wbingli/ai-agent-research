# Tech Digest — 2026-06-27

**1. DSpark: Speculative decoding accelerates LLM inference**
DeepSeek’s DSpark paper/codebase focuses on making speculative decoding practical across training and evaluation, which matters because inference cost and latency are now core product constraints for AI teams. Engineering leaders should watch this as another sign that serving efficiency is becoming as strategic as model quality. [Read](https://github.com/deepseek-ai/DeepSpec/blob/main/DSpark_paper.pdf)

**3. A peek into Reddit’s anti-spam internals**
This writeup examines an accidental leak of Reddit’s “spamurai” anti-abuse system, offering a rare look at how a large consumer platform combines signals, rules, and enforcement workflows. It is useful context for leaders building trust-and-safety, fraud, or abuse-prevention systems where adversaries adapt quickly. [Read](https://lyra.horse/blog/2026/06/reddit-spam-internals/)

**4. Why ClickHouse rewrote WAL-G for Postgres backups in Rust**
ClickHouse introduced WAL-RUS, a Rust rewrite of WAL-G for PostgreSQL backups that it says cuts virtual memory usage by more than 70% while preserving compatibility. The broader takeaway is that mature infrastructure tools still have room for major reliability and resource-efficiency wins when rewritten around tighter runtime constraints. [Read](https://clickhouse.com/blog/walrus-postgres-backups-in-rust)

**5. Linux Foundation launches Akrites for AI-enabled open-source security threats**
Akrites aims to coordinate protection for critical open-source software against AI-amplified cyber threats. For engineering organizations, this points to a likely shift from ad hoc dependency hygiene toward more formal ecosystem-level defense, provenance, and response processes. [Read](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/press/linux-foundation-and-industry-leaders-launch-akrites-to-defend-critical-open-source-software-against-ai-enabled-cyber-threats)
