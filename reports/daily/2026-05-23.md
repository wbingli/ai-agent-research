# Tech Digest — 2026-05-23

**1. You can’t afford to lead agentic engineering from the sidelines**
AI-assisted development is shifting from tooling choice to operating-model change: leaders need hands-on experience with agentic workflows before redesigning teams, reviews, and delivery metrics. Useful framing for deciding where automation should accelerate engineering versus where judgment and accountability stay human. [Read](https://blog.kilo.ai/p/you-cant-afford-to-lead-agentic-engineering)

**2. Making deep learning go brrrr from first principles**
A clear walkthrough of why deep learning performance comes down to memory movement, parallelism, kernels, and hardware-aware math rather than just “use a GPU.” It is a strong refresher for leaders evaluating inference cost, accelerator utilization, and whether optimization work belongs in-house. [Read](https://horace.io/brrr_intro.html)

**3. Gnutella: A protocol outliving the world that created it**
This retrospective explains how Gnutella’s decentralized peer discovery, query flooding, and later ultrapeer design kept a peer-to-peer network alive long after its original era. The lessons still matter for resilient distributed systems: topology, incentives, and failure modes can dominate elegance. [Read](https://rickcarlino.com/notes/p2p/gnutella-explanation.html)

**4. Pardon MIE?**
A security writeup claims Apple’s new Memory Integrity Enforcement on M5-class hardware was bypassed quickly, digging into what the bug was and what it means for exploit writers and defenders. Even if the details are specialized, it is a reminder that hardware-backed mitigations still need adversarial validation and rapid patch loops. [Read](https://ironpeak.be/blog/bypassing-apple-mie/)

**5. .NET finally gets union types**
.NET 11 preview adds C# union types, making it easier to model “one of several shapes” directly instead of relying on inheritance, enums-plus-payloads, or ad hoc wrappers. For backend teams, this can reduce invalid states in domain models and make API/result handling more explicit. [Read](https://andrewlock.net/exploring-the-dotnet-11-preview-2-dotnet-gets-union-types/)
