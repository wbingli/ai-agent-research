# Tech Digest — 2026-06-14

**1. Homebrew 6.0.0**
Homebrew 6.0 adds tap-trust security, a faster internal JSON API, Linux sandboxing, bundle improvements, and early macOS 27 support. For engineering leaders, it is a reminder that developer-platform supply-chain controls and package-manager performance are now core productivity infrastructure. [Read](https://brew.sh/2026/06/11/homebrew-6.0.0/)

**2. The future of Siri, or: why private inference isn’t private enough**
Matthew Green argues that “private cloud inference” still leaves unsolved risks around agent authority, personal data access, and what voice assistants can do on a user’s behalf. This is a useful framing for teams adding agents to products: privacy architecture is not enough unless capability boundaries and auditability are designed in. [Read](https://blog.cryptographyengineering.com/2026/06/09/apples-siri-ai-or-more-shouting-into-the-void-about-private-agents/)

**3. The only scalable delete in Postgres is DROP TABLE**
PlanetScale’s writeup explains why large deletes create vacuum, replication, locking, and index-maintenance pain, and why retention should often be modeled around partitions that can be dropped or truncated. It is a practical database-architecture lesson for any team with high-volume data lifecycle requirements. [Read](https://planetscale.com/blog/the-only-scalable-delete)

**4. TorchCodec 0.14: HDR Video Decoding for CPU and CUDA, and Fast Wav Decoder**
Meta’s TorchCodec release adds HDR video decoding on CPU/CUDA and a faster WAV decoder for PyTorch media pipelines. This matters for ML and video teams because efficient decoding is often the hidden bottleneck between raw media, training data, and inference throughput. [Read](https://github.com/meta-pytorch/torchcodec/releases/tag/v0.14.0)

**5. Pyodide 314.0: WebAssembly wheels for PyPI**
Pyodide 314.0 moves Python-in-the-browser packaging forward with WebAssembly wheel support tied to the accepted Emscripten packaging PEP. It points toward a more standard path for shipping serious Python tooling into browsers, notebooks, and edge-like client environments. [Read](https://blog.pyodide.org/posts/314-release/)
