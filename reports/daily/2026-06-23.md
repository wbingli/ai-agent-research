# Tech Digest — 2026-06-23

**1. Unlimited OCR: One-shot long-horizon parsing**
Baidu’s new OCR project targets long, complex document parsing in a single pass, pointing toward document AI systems that can handle full reports, manuals, and workflows without brittle chunking. For engineering leaders, this is another sign that multimodal foundation models are moving from demos into practical back-office automation. [Read](https://github.com/baidu/Unlimited-OCR)

**2. The Coming Loop**
Armin Ronacher argues that AI coding tools are pushing teams toward tighter “loops”: harnesses, evals, automated checks, and feedback systems that make agentic development reliable. The takeaway is that productivity gains will depend less on prompting and more on engineering the verification loop around the agent. [Read](https://lucumr.pocoo.org/2026/6/23/the-coming-loop/)

**3. Keeping the Web Open and Private in the Bot Era**
Mozilla and major browser vendors are working with Cloudflare on privacy-preserving ways to prove traffic is not malicious without endless CAPTCHAs or invasive fingerprinting. This matters because AI agents will increase automated traffic, and the web needs bot defenses that do not break privacy or legitimate automation. [Read](https://blog.mozilla.org/en/privacy-security/keeping-the-web-open-and-private-in-the-bot-era/)

**4. Why American data centers can’t plug in**
The AI data-center buildout is increasingly constrained not by total electricity supply, but by interconnection queues, transmission bottlenecks, and grid planning. Infrastructure leaders should read this as a reminder that AI scaling is now a power-delivery and permitting problem as much as a GPU procurement problem. [Read](https://worksinprogress.co/issue/why-american-data-centers-cant-plug-in/)

**5. Vulnerability Reports Are Not Special Anymore**
Filippo Valsorda argues that LLMs are commoditizing vulnerability discovery and changing the signal-to-noise ratio of security reports. Security programs will need better triage, clearer disclosure paths, and more automation because the old model of treating every report as scarce expert labor is breaking down. [Read](https://words.filippo.io/vuln-reports/)
