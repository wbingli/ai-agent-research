# Tech Digest — 2026-07-03

**1. Leanstral 1.5: Proof Abundance for All**
Mistral released a new model family aimed at formal mathematical reasoning and proof generation, signaling that specialized LLMs are moving from chat assistants toward verifiable engineering and research workflows. For leaders, the interesting part is not just benchmark gains but the direction: domain-tuned models that can automate parts of high-assurance reasoning. [Read](https://mistral.ai/news/leanstral-1-5/)

**2. GLM5.2 on AMD MI355X at 2626 tok/s/node**
Wafer reports strong inference throughput for GLM5.2 on AMD’s MI355X, claiming materially better cost performance than Blackwell-class alternatives for this workload. If reproducible, this matters for AI infrastructure planning because accelerator optionality can reduce vendor lock-in and reshape serving economics. [Read](https://www.wafer.ai/blog/glm52-amd)

**3. Everything I know about running LLMs locally**
James O’Beirne published a practical guide to local LLM hardware, quantization, runtimes, and tradeoffs. It is useful for engineering teams evaluating when private, offline, or edge inference is good enough versus when hosted frontier models are worth the latency, cost, and data-governance burden. [Read](https://github.com/jamesob/local-llm)

**4. Hunting a 16-year-old SQLite WAL bug with TLA+**
Canonical walks through modeling a long-lived SQLite write-ahead-log race with TLA+, reproducing the failure, validating the fix, and checking whether dqlite is affected. It is a strong example of formal methods being practical for real storage and distributed-systems reliability, not just academic proofs. [Read](https://ubuntu.com/blog/hunting-a-16-year-old-sqlite-bug-with-tla-is-dqlite-affected)

**5. PostgreSQL and the OOM Killer: Why We Use Strict Memory Overcommit**
Ubicloud explains how strict Linux memory overcommit can protect PostgreSQL from catastrophic OOM kills, plus the operational edge cases that forced them to tune it carefully. This is a good reminder that database reliability often depends on kernel-level resource policy, not only query tuning or bigger instances. [Read](https://www.ubicloud.com/blog/postgresql-and-the-oom-killer-why-we-use-strict-memory-overcommit)
