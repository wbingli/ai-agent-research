### 4. Google's Bayesian Teaching for LLMs
**Source:** [Google Research](https://radicaldatascience.wordpress.com/2026/03/05/ai-news-briefs-bulletin-board-for-march-2026/), [Medium writeup](https://medium.com/modelmind/teaching-ai-to-think-in-probabilities-how-google-trains-llms-to-reason-like-bayesians-1d5408f33231)

Google researchers introduced **Bayesian Teaching** — a training method that teaches LLMs to update probabilities when new evidence appears:
- Trains models to reason like Bayesian agents, updating belief distributions as they observe data
- Compared against human participants and a mathematically optimal "Bayesian Assistant"
- Tested on Gemma and Qwen models

**Why it matters:** Addresses a fundamental LLM weakness — current models are notoriously bad at updating beliefs given new evidence. If this approach generalizes, it could significantly improve agent decision-making in dynamic environments.
