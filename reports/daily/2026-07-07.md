# Tech Digest — 2026-07-07

**1. AI Meets Cryptography: What AI Found in Cloudflare’s CIRCL**
A security team ran an AI-assisted audit pipeline against Cloudflare’s CIRCL cryptography library and surfaced real bug classes, showing where LLM-driven review can augment expert security work. For engineering leaders, the takeaway is not “replace auditors,” but start treating AI audit workflows as another repeatable layer in defense-in-depth. **2. You Shouldn’t Trust Trusted Publishing**
William Woodruff argues that “trusted publishing” for package releases still leaves subtle supply-chain trust gaps, especially around identity, repository control, and CI boundaries. If your org relies on PyPI/GitHub automation, this is a useful reminder to threat-model the whole release path rather than just removing static tokens. [Read](https://blog.yossarian.net/2026/07/07/You-shouldnt-trust-trusted-publishing)

**3. Why We Built Yet Another Postgres Connection Pooler**
PgDog explains why they built a Postgres pooler that also handles load balancing and sharding, aiming to scale Postgres horizontally without forcing application rewrites. It’s a practical look at the pressure points teams hit when “just use Postgres” becomes a high-throughput distributed-systems problem. [Read](https://pgdog.dev/blog/why-yet-another-connection-pooler)

**4. Local, CPU-Friendly, High-Quality TTS with Kokoro**
This writeup shows how Kokoro can deliver realistic text-to-speech locally on CPU, making private and low-cost voice generation much more accessible. That matters for product teams because voice interfaces no longer have to imply cloud inference, GPU dependency, or sensitive-audio data exposure. [Read](https://ariya.io/2026/03/local-cpu-friendly-high-quality-tts-text-to-speech-with-kokoro/)

**5. Nvidia’s Next-Gen AI Rack System Reportedly Delayed**
CNBC reports that Nvidia’s next AI rack platform is delayed until 2028 due to manufacturing snags, adding evidence that datacenter AI roadmaps are constrained by physical supply chains, not just model demand. Capacity planning for AI products should assume hardware cadence risk, power/cooling limits, and vendor schedule slips. [Read](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/06/nvidia-kyber-rack-system-delays-manufacturing-taiwan-rubin-chips-.html)
