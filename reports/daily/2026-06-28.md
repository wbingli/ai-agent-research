# Tech Digest — 2026-06-28

**1. GLM 5.2 beats Claude in cyber benchmarks**
Semgrep reports that Zhipu’s open-weight GLM 5.2 outperformed Claude Opus 4.8 on its cybersecurity benchmark when models were given only a prompt. For engineering leaders, the signal is that capable open models are becoming realistic options for security automation, but benchmark design and operational guardrails matter more than brand names. [Read](https://semgrep.dev/blog/2026/we-have-mythos-at-home-glm-52-beats-claude-in-our-cyber-benchmarks/)

**2. Model Training as Code**
Aleph Alpha describes Savanna, a “model factory” that turns complex model-training pipelines into versioned, repeatable code across teams and stages. The important takeaway is organizational: as training gets more specialized, reproducibility, orchestration, and ownership become platform problems, not just research problems. [Read](https://aleph-alpha.com/en/blog/model-training-as-code/)

**3. A peek into Reddit’s anti-spam internals**
This writeup examines how Reddit accidentally exposed details of its anti-spam system, including signals and enforcement logic. It is a useful reminder that abuse-prevention systems are themselves sensitive infrastructure: leaks can help attackers tune around defenses and should be threat-modeled like any other production secret. [Read](https://lyra.horse/blog/2026/06/reddit-spam-internals/)

**4. How VictoriaLogs stores your logs in a columnar layout**
VictoriaMetrics explains the on-disk design behind VictoriaLogs: streams, daily partitions, immutable parts, blocks, and columnar files. For backend and infra teams, it is a practical look at why log systems increasingly borrow database-storage ideas to improve compression, query speed, and operational cost. [Read](https://victoriametrics.com/blog/victorialogs-internals-columnar-storage-on-disk/)

**5. OpenAI Codex still lacks a sensitive-file exclusion mechanism**
A GitHub issue asks for repo-level and global ignore rules so coding agents cannot read or send sensitive paths such as secrets, customer data, or private notes. This is a small feature request with big governance implications: agent adoption in engineering teams depends on enforceable data boundaries, not just trust in prompts. [Read](https://github.com/openai/codex/issues/2847)
