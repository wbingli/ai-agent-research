# Tech Digest — 2026-06-08

**1. MiMo-v2.5-Pro-UltraSpeed: 1T model with 1000 tokens per second**
Xiaomi says it pushed a 1T-parameter model past 1,000 tokens/sec on commodity GPUs through model-system co-design, a reminder that inference architecture—not just model quality—is becoming a core competitive lever. For engineering leaders, this is a useful signal on where serving-cost breakthroughs may come from next. [Read](https://mimo.xiaomi.com/blog/mimo-tilert-1000tps)

**2. Apple reveals new AI architecture built around Google Gemini models**
Apple is reportedly rebuilding Apple Intelligence around Gemini-derived foundation models adapted for both on-device execution and Private Cloud Compute. The strategic takeaway is that platform AI is shifting toward hybrid deployment, privacy-preserving server fallback, and developer-facing model APIs. [Read](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/08/apple-reveals-new-ai-architecture/)

**3. xAI is looking more like a datacentre REIT than a frontier lab**
This analysis argues xAI’s near-term advantage may be GPU capacity and datacenter economics, with rented compute to other frontier labs becoming as important as model differentiation. It’s a good framing for AI infrastructure planning: scarce power, chips, and facilities can shape the market as much as research velocity. [Read](https://martinalderson.com/posts/xais-new-rental-business/)

**4. Vulnerability and malware checks in uv**
Astral added `uv audit` for Python dependency vulnerabilities plus experimental malware detection during installs. This matters because Python package management is consolidating around faster tools, and security scanning is moving earlier into the developer workflow instead of being left to CI or separate SCA systems. [Read](https://astral.sh/blog/uv-audit)

**5. Self-hosting email the hard way from your own routable IPv4 block up**
Anil Madhavapeddy documents running email from a RIPE-allocated IPv4 block with Postfix, rspamd, Dovecot, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. It’s a practical deep dive into the operational reality behind “simple” internet infrastructure: reputation, deliverability, DNS, and abuse controls are the real system. [Read](https://anil.recoil.org/notes/recoil-self-hosting-2026)
