# Tech Digest — 2026-07-17

**1. The State of Open Source AI**
A new survey maps where open-source AI stands in mid-2026, from model capability and licensing to tooling and deployment patterns. For engineering leaders, it is a useful snapshot of what can be self-hosted or adapted without depending entirely on closed model vendors. [Read](https://stateofopensource.ai/)

**2. Kimi K3, and what we can still learn from the pelican benchmark**
Simon Willison examines Moonshot AI’s new 2.8T-parameter Kimi K3 and uses a deceptively simple benchmark to show how model evaluations still reveal practical failure modes. The takeaway is that leaders should test models against real product workflows, not just leaderboard claims. [Read](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/kimi-k3/)

**3. Linus Torvalds on LLM usage in kernel development**
Torvalds rejects a blanket ban on AI-assisted kernel contributions, putting the burden on maintainers to judge patches by quality rather than tooling. This is a strong signal for software organizations: AI coding policy needs review discipline, provenance awareness, and accountability, not ideology. [Read](https://lore.kernel.org/linux-media/CAHk-=wi4zC+Ze8e+p3tMv8TtG_80KzsZ1syL9anBtmEh5Z40vg@mail.gmail.com/)

**4. What can we learn from Bun’s rapid Rust rewrite with AI?**
The Pragmatic Engineer looks at Bun’s AI-assisted Zig-to-Rust migration, reportedly compressing a 1–2 year rewrite into 11 days at a cost of about $165K. The lesson is not “AI rewrites are easy,” but that strong tests, tight scope, and expert review can turn AI into a migration accelerator. [Read](https://blog.pragmaticengineer.com/the-pulse-what-can-we-learn-from-buns-rapid-rust-rewrite-with-ai/)

**5. Learning a few things about running SQLite**
Julia Evans shares practical notes from running SQLite in production-like settings, covering operational details that are easy to miss when treating it as “just a file.” It matters because SQLite keeps expanding into edge, local-first, and small-service architectures where simplicity still needs operational rigor. [Read](https://jvns.ca/blog/2026/07/17/learning-about-running-sqlite/)
